Lore & Trivia

Ceruledge emerged when Charcadet bonded with cursed armor, its flaming blades fueled by the spirit of a fallen knight. Its molten strikes and spectral embers make it a formidable foe and a prized narrative piece for collectors seeking depth and drama in their TCG lineup.

Competitive Role & Collecting Tips

In Standard play, Ceruledge AR shines as a potent disruption engine. Its “Cursed Edge” Ability can strip all Special Energy from your opponent’s Active Pokémon, often swinging the momentum in your favor by severing their combo lines. Against energy-heavy decks like Blacephalon or Zacian, this effect buys precious turns. Pair Ceruledge with Stadium cards such as Chaos Tower or Viridian Forest to lock down Energy attachments further. In Expanded, it finds niche play alongside “Rillaboom V” for accelerating Grass Energy to fuel Cursed Edge early.

On the collector’s front, Ceruledge AR #197/191—Illustration Rare by Rond— currently trades between $8–$12 USD (June 2025), with spikes after high-visibility tournament wins or major set reprints. Check live pricing and completed sales on TCGplayer, eBay, and community trackers like PokémonPrices. Limited promos or alt-art prints can fetch 20–30% premiums.

To preserve your IR foil finish, sleeve Ceruledge in ultra-clear penny sleeves, then top-load into rigid holders—avoid PVC-based sleeves that can leach plasticizers. Store cards upright in a cool, dark place with humidity below 50% to prevent whitening on the edges and maintain resale value. If you’re building a binder display, use acid-free pages and keep away from direct sunlight to protect both art and foil sheen.
